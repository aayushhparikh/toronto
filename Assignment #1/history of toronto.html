History

Before Europeans arrived, to present-day toronto the land was occupied by the iroquois which was taken from the huron centuries before, The name toronto is likely derived from the Iroquoian word tkaronto, which means “place where trees stand in the water” this is referring to the northern end of what is now lake simcoe. In 1793, John Graves Simcoe established the town of York on the Toronto Purchase lands, naming it after Prince Frederick, Duke of York and Albany. In 1813, as part of the war of 1812, the Battle of York ended in the town’s capture and plunder by the US forces, the American forces destroyed much of the town including the parliament buildings. The British forces later retaliated by burning the White House during the burning of Washington D.C. By 1834, York was incorporated as the City of Toronto on March 6, 1834 during this time the name was also reverted back to the original name. Toronto’s population at the time was 9000 people which included african-american slaves. For brief periods Toronto was the capital of Canada,

  1. 1849-1852
  2. 1856-1858
  3. After this 1858, Quebec was the capital until 1866
afterwich ottawa become the capital and has remained the capital since. Toronto became the capital of Ontario soon after its official creation in 1867. In the 19th century the city build an extensive sewage system to improve sanitation, streets were illuminated with gas lighting as a regular service. Long-distance railway lines were constructed, including a route completed in 1854 connecting Toronto to the Upper Great Lakes. The Grand Trunk Railway and the Northern Railway of Canada joined in the building of the first Union Station in Downtown. Toronto became the largest alcohol distillation centre in North America. The Great Toronto Fire of 1904 destroyed a large section of Downtown Toronto, and it caused more than $10 million in damage, but the city quickly rebuilt it. Toronto became known as a centre where immigrants could migrate to and would prosper.
